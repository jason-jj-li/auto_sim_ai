Biomarker Details

The CMD-BA-CNC project focuses on the harmonization and analysis of 6 core cardiometabolic biomarkers

Hemoglobin A1c

HbA1c

Glycated hemoglobin reflecting average blood glucose levels over 2-3 months. Key indicator for diabetes diagnosis and glycemic control.

Normal Range < 5.7%
Diabetes ≥ 6.5%

C-Reactive Protein

CRP

Inflammatory marker produced by liver in response to inflammation. Elevated levels indicate increased cardiovascular risk.

Low Risk < 1.0 mg/L
High Risk > 3.0 mg/L

Total Cholesterol

TC

Total amount of cholesterol in blood, including both LDL and HDL. Key lipid marker for cardiovascular disease risk assessment.

Desirable < 200 mg/dL
High ≥ 240 mg/dL

HDL Cholesterol

HDL

High-density lipoprotein cholesterol, often called "good cholesterol". Higher levels are protective against heart disease.

Men > 40 mg/dL
Women > 50 mg/dL

Blood Pressure

SBP/DBP

Systolic and diastolic blood pressure measurements. Critical indicators for cardiovascular health and hypertension diagnosis.

Normal < 120/80 mmHg
Hypertension ≥ 140/90 mmHg

Body Mass Index

BMI

Body mass index calculated from height and weight measurements. Key anthropometric indicator for obesity assessment.

Normal 18.5-24.9 kg/m²
Obese ≥ 30 kg/m²

Harmonization Strategy

Laboratory Standardization

Ensuring consistent laboratory methods and calibration across all participating studies to minimize technical variance in biomarker measurements.

  • Standardized assay protocols
  • Quality control procedures
  • Inter-laboratory validation
Statistical Harmonization

Advanced statistical methods to account for population differences and study-specific effects while preserving biological variation.

  • Population standardization
  • Batch effect correction
  • Meta-analytical approaches

Data Collection Methods

Dried Blood Spots (DBS)

Capillary blood collection on filter paper for HbA1c, CRP, and lipid analysis in field settings.

Venous Blood Draw

Traditional blood collection method for comprehensive biomarker panel analysis in clinical settings.

Physical Measurements

Standardized protocols for blood pressure, height, and weight measurements by trained personnel.

Quality Assurance

Data Quality Controls
  • Range validation and outlier detection
  • Missing data pattern analysis
  • Cross-study consistency checks
Documentation Standards
  • Detailed metadata documentation
  • Protocol standardization records
  • Data provenance tracking
