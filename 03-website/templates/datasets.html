Comprehensive Dataset Information

Complete technical specifications and collection protocols for CMD-BA-CNC biomarker datasets

Core Biomarker Panel

The CMD-BA-CNC project harmonizes 6 essential cardiometabolic biomarkers across international aging studies:

Hemoglobin A1c (HbA1c)

Long-term glucose control indicator

Normal: < 5.7% Diabetes: ≥ 6.5% Units: % of total hemoglobin
C-Reactive Protein (CRP)

Systemic inflammation marker

Low risk: < 1.0 mg/L High risk: > 3.0 mg/L Units: mg/L
Total Cholesterol

Complete lipid profile marker

Desirable: < 200 mg/dL High: ≥ 240 mg/dL Units: mg/dL or mmol/L
HDL Cholesterol

"Good" cholesterol, cardioprotective

Men: > 40 mg/dL Women: > 50 mg/dL Units: mg/dL or mmol/L
Blood Pressure (SBP/DBP)

Cardiovascular health indicator

Normal: < 120/80 mmHg Stage 1 HTN: 130-139/80-89 mmHg Units: mmHg
BMI (Body Mass Index)

Anthropometric obesity indicator

Normal: 18.5-24.9 kg/m² Obese: ≥ 30 kg/m² Units: kg/m²

Data Collection Protocols

Dried Blood Spots (DBS)

Capillary blood collection on filter paper, enabling field-based biomarker analysis.

BP Protocol: Standard measurement protocols across studies

Biomarkers: HbA1c, CRP, Total Cholesterol, HDL

Advantages: Minimally invasive, stable at room temperature

Storage: -20°C to -80°C for long-term

Venous Blood Collection

Traditional phlebotomy for comprehensive biomarker panels in clinical settings.

Fasting: 8-12 hours for lipid measurements

Processing: Centrifugation within 2 hours

Storage: Plasma/serum at -80°C

Quality: Higher precision for some analytes

Study-Specific Collection Protocols

HRS (United States)

Method: DBS collection (2006-2016)

Biomarkers: Complete 6-marker panel

Protocol: Alternating half-sample design

Status: Transitioned to venous blood post-2016

ELSA (England)

Method: Nurse visits with venous blood

Biomarkers: Complete 6-marker panel

Protocol: Standardized clinical procedures

Quality: High precision laboratory analysis

SHARE (Europe)

Method: DBS collection (Wave 6, 2015)

Limitation: NO measured blood pressure data

Biomarkers: HbA1c, CRP, Cholesterol, BMI

Coverage: 4 of 6 markers available

CHARLS (China)

Method: Venous blood collection

Biomarkers: Complete 6-marker panel

Waves: 2011, 2015 biomarker collection

Coverage: National representative sample

Data Limitations and Harmonization Challenges

Study-Specific Limitations
SHARE:

• No measured blood pressure data available

• Limited to 4 of 6 core biomarkers

HAALSI:

• No HbA1c - capillary glucose only

• CRP available in Wave 1 only

IFLS:

• Severely limited: NO Cholesterol, BP, BMI, physical measures

Harmonization Solutions
  • Statistical calibration between DBS and venous methods
  • Population-specific reference ranges
  • Meta-analytical approaches for missing data
  • Quality control standardization

Quality Assurance and Data Validation

Pre-analytical QC
  • Standardized collection protocols
  • Fasting requirements (8-12 hours)
  • Temperature-controlled storage
  • Time-to-processing documentation
Analytical QC
  • Certified reference materials
  • Inter-laboratory validation
  • Calibration traceability
  • Precision and accuracy monitoring
Post-analytical QC
  • Outlier detection algorithms
  • Range validation checks
  • Cross-study consistency analysis
  • Missing data pattern assessment
