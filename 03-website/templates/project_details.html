Project Overview: CMD-BA-CNC

CardioMetabolic Disease-Biological Aging: Cross-National Comparisons

A comprehensive international research initiative harmonizing biomarker data across multiple longitudinal aging studies to enable cross-national comparisons of cardiometabolic health and biological aging processes.

Primary Objectives
  • Harmonize cardiometabolic biomarker data across 11 international aging studies
  • Develop standardized biological aging indicators for cross-national analysis
  • Enable comparative research on aging patterns across diverse populations
  • Create accessible datasets for the international research community
Key Statistics
Studies Included: 11
Countries Covered: 15+
Core Biomarkers: 6
Total Participants: ~150,000
Time Span: 1992-2024

Participating International Studies

CHARLS (China)

Full Name: China Health and Retirement Study

Period: 2011-ongoing

Sample: ~21,000 adults 45+

Biomarkers: Waves 1(2011) & 3(2015)

LASI-DAD (India)

Sample: ~6,000 adults 60+

MHAS (Mexico)

Research Applications and Impact

Cardiovascular Health

Cross-national comparisons of cardiovascular disease risk factors and their associations with aging outcomes.

Biological Aging

Development of standardized biological age indicators using cardiometabolic biomarker panels.

Population Differences

Understanding how genetic, environmental, and cultural factors influence aging across diverse populations.
